What is Health Insurance?

It is an agreement between the insurer and the policyholder whereby an insurance company agrees to reimburse for medical costs incurred by the policyholder during the tenure of the policy. According to the policy terms, the insured may incur medical expenses if they get ill or meet an unfortunate accident that leads to treatment at the hospital. To avail of the coverage benefits of the policy, the policyholder must pay a specific amount periodically, called a premium.

The premium is determined by the insurance company and must be paid by the policyholders without any fail either monthly, quarterly, half-yearly or annually.

How To File A Health Insurance Claim?

Claim under a health insurance policy can be made in two ways - in a cashless manner and in a reimbursement manner. A cashless claim is when you receive treatment at a network hospital of the insurance provider who is servicing your policy. If the claim request is accepted, the insurance company directly settles the bills at the hospital. Whereas in reimbursement claims, you are required to pay for the hospital bill from your own pocket first, and make a claim request with the insurance company later. The insurance company will process your claim and reimburse you for the same after accepting the claim. You can make a health insurance claim by following the simple steps given below:

  • Step 1: Visit the network hospital or the hospital of your choice and inform the insurance company about the hospitalisation.
  • Step 2: Fill the pre-authorization form at the hospitalâ€™s TPA (third-party administrator) desk.
  • Step 3: You will receive an approval letter by the claim management team.
  • Step 4: Respond to the query raised by the claim management team, if any.
  • Step 5: Avail the required medical treatment.
  • Step 6: The insurance company will directly settle the bills at the network hospital as per the terms and conditions mentioned in your policy.
